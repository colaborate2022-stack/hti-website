Housekeeping Done Right!

Squeaky clean. Awesome fragrance. Inviting vibe. That's what we expect in a restaurant or hotel, right? Throw in gorgeous fresh flower arrangements — and anyone would swoon! When you get all this and some more at a hotel or restaurant you visit, you know that their Housekeeping Department (HKD) is doing their job right. When you don't — well, you know who needs training. Guest experience, especially women guests, relies heavily on the HKD. Train your HKD team and then watch your guests' happiness go through the roof! Here's how to get it right:

Words by Team HTI
Date 09-12-2023
Reading Time 03 min read
SOPs, The Lifesaver

A manual documenting precise Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) for all activities is very important. From cleaning guest rooms, restaurants, lobby and public areas; to changing table and bed linen, cleaning bathroom fixtures, the works. It's a good idea to video record the SOPs and dub it in multiple languages, so that all staff members can refer to it with ease. Regularly update this guide and ensure your staff implements it.

Get Hands-On

While theoretical knowledge is essential, hands-on training is equally important. Arrange practical training sessions where staff can apply the knowledge gained from the SOP manual. This could include practising room cleaning, making beds, and handling cleaning equipment efficiently.

Stay Safe

Housekeeping staff work with cleaning chemicals and equipment that are hazardous. Ensure that they receive proper safety training, including how to use protective gear, handle chemicals safely, and what to do in case of accidents. Safety training is not just about compliance; it's about the wellbeing of your staff.

Customer Is King

Training your HKD staff in Customer Service ensures that staff interact politely and professionally with guests, and are responsive to their requests. A smile, a polite greeting, or a swift response to a guest's need can make a world of difference in their overall experience.

Tik-Tok, Tik-Tok

Training should focus on time management, helping staff complete tasks within the allocated timeframe. Teach them to prioritize tasks, and get the work done. Efficient time management ensures that all areas are cleaned and prepared on time, contributing to smooth operations and guest satisfaction.

TQC, My Friend?

Implement a system for quality control checks. Senior staff or supervisors should regularly inspect cleaned areas to ensure they meet the established standards. This not only ensures quality but also helps identify areas where additional training might be needed.

Communicate To Win

Train HKD staff to report any issues, maintenance needs, or special guest requests promptly. Effective communication resolves problems swiftly, ensuring a seamless guest experience. Encourage open communication between team members and with other departments.

Speak The Guest's Language

In today's global hospitality market, it's beneficial to offer multilingual training. This can help staff communicate with guests from different cultural backgrounds, enhancing the guest experience. Even basic phrases in a few languages can go a long way in making guests feel welcome and understood.

Are You Eco-Friendly?

Train your staff on sustainable cleaning methods and the importance of reducing waste, conserving resources, and recycling. As guests become more environmentally conscious, having an eco-friendly housekeeping team can be a significant plus point for your establishment.

Train On…

Provide opportunities for ongoing training and development to keep your HKD staff updated with the latest trends, technologies, and best practices. This could include workshops, seminars, or online courses. Continuous learning keeps your team motivated and ensures that your housekeeping standards remain high.
